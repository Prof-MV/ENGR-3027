Text: Reid & Sanders (Chapter 10: Pages 370 ‚Äì 379)

6.1 Designing Product Layouts (Steps)

  1. Identify tasks & immediate predecessors (Precedence Diagram).
  2. Determine output rate (Units/Time).
  3. Determine cycle time (\(C = \frac{\text{Available Time}}{\text{Desired Output}}\)).
  4. Compute Theoretical Minimum (TM) number of Stations (\(TM = \frac{\sum t}{C}\)).
  5. Assign tasks to workstations (Balance the line).
  6. Compute efficiency, idle time & balance delay.

6.1.1 Example: Vicki‚Äôs Pizzeria

  • Total Task Time (\(\sum t\)): 165 seconds.
  • Desired Output: 60 units/hr.
  • Cycle Time (\(C\)): 60 seconds.
  • TM Stations: \(165 / 60 = 2.75 \rightarrow 3\) stations.
  • Efficiency: \(\frac{\sum t}{n \times C} = \frac{165}{3 \times 60} = 91.7\%\).
