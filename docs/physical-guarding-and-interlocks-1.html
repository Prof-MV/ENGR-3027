Chapter 8 1. Physical Guarding and Interlocks

Physical barriers are the most intuitive form of protection. They create a ‚Äúhard‚Äù separation between the operator and moving parts.

  • Fixed Guards: Permanent panels (often wire mesh or polycarbonate) that require tools to remove.
  • Interlocked Gates: Access points equipped with sensors. When the gate is opened, the safety controller initiates a Category 0 (immediate) or Category 1 (controlled) stop.
  • Trapped Key Systems (LOTO): A mechanical sequence ensuring that a machine cannot be energized unless the access key is returned to the control panel, ensuring no one is inside the cell during start-up.
